Portes Ouvertes

      • From loud secret to silent opening
      • Europan 16
      • With Gilles Caron and Pauline Delorme
      • 2021

      Situated between the linear territorial structures of the river Meuse, the railway and the departmental road, the abandoned military site of Sart Hulet turns away from the activities of the rest of Namur. The separation from the city is amplified by a shifting topography placing the complex on an intermediate plateau. This combination has probably favored the installation of military activities here, completing the erasure of this fragment from the daily life of the city. Paradoxically, the secrecy of military activities did not make the place fall into silence. On the contrary, the barbed wire fence and blurred aerial photos communicate the hidden character of the place to the world. The place is secret and everyone knows it.

      • From loud secret to silent opening
      • Europan 16
      • With Gilles Caron and Pauline Delorme
      • 2021

      Portes Ouvertes proposes to valorize the existing "mystery capital" accumulated on the site, opposing the approach of the municipality suggesting to transform the site into a recreational space reminiscent of an amusement park. Instead, inhabitants of Namur are to be invited to discover the site for simply what it is: a "terra incognita". To do this, a strategy of appropriation for Sart Hulet is initiated by an extremely discreet gesture: unlocking the doors of the fence surrounding the site.

      • From loud secret to silent opening
      • Europan 16
      • With Gilles Caron and Pauline Delorme
      • 2021

      Starting from this opening, a calendar divides the appropriation into six concrete actions: opening, await, observation, accompaniment, inhabitation and extrapolation. These are translated into spatialities and gestures on the site like unlocking the doors, terrain vague, uses, territory and strategy. A series of representations illustrate possible appropriations of the site, based on the existing spatial configurations. These images, inhabited by a crowd of diverse protagonists, bring together uses that take place in the different temporalities of the calendar. A staging that embraces the diversity and complexity of potential experiences.
