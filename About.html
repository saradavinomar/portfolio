      Confront is the body of work produced by myself, Sara Davin Omar, often through collaborative processes. I am interested in architecture as an emancipatory practice, which requires an understanding of the intersecting power structures that have and still do shape the built. To explicitly be positioned against is therefore an integral part of my creative process.

      I hold a BSc degree in architecture from the Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm (SE) and a MSc degree in architecture with a specialization on large-scale projects from the ​​École polytechnique fédérale de Lausanne (CH). Currently I am enrolled at the research-based postmaster course Decolonizing Architecture at the Royal Institute of Art in Stockholm (SE).

      Starting from this opening, a calendar divides the appropriation into six concrete actions: opening, await, observation, accompaniment, inhabitation and extrapolation. These are translated into spatialities and gestures on the site like unlocking the doors, terrain vague, uses, territory and strategy. A series of representations illustrate possible appropriations of the site, based on the existing spatial configurations. These images, inhabited by a crowd of diverse protagonists, bring together uses that take place in the different temporalities of the calendar. A staging that embraces the diversity and complexity of potential experiences.

      Open doors means offering almost nothing so that a lot can change. It means upholding a critical position vis-à-vis the master plan as a “neutral” tool to conceive a project on the urban scale. It means avoiding to speak in terms of programs, activities or sectors but rather of uses, experiences and territories. It means widening the biodiversity of the site by acknowledging that life is a flux of changes.
